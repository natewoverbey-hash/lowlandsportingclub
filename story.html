Our Story

How it started. Why it matters.

The Beginning

It started with a conversation about what was missing.

If you shoot sporting clays in the Charleston area, you know the problem. The closest option is forty-five minutes up the interstate to a club that — by its own members' admission — succeeds in spite of itself. The next nearest is an hour and a half. For a region with one of the fastest-growing populations in the Southeast, the gap is remarkable.

Nathan Hull and Preston Hull saw the same gap from different angles. Nathan spent fourteen years in enterprise technology, building relationships with executives across the Southeast. Preston grew up in the Lowcountry, raised on the shooting sports, with a deep understanding of what makes a club worth belonging to. Together, they asked a simple question: what would a sporting club look like if it were built the way the best things in the Lowcountry are built — with care, with restraint, and with respect for what was here before?

The Vision

Not a gun range.
Not a country club.
A sporting club in the oldest sense.

Lowland Sporting Club is built on a belief that the best experiences come from places where the land, the sport, and the people who gather there are inseparable from one another. The sixty-acre property on the Edisto River was chosen because it already had the character that most clubs spend decades trying to manufacture — mature oaks, natural water features, existing roads and infrastructure, and the kind of quiet that city people drive an hour to find.

The property, looking east
The Principle

Small enough to matter.
Rooted enough to last.

The club is limited to 125–175 members across its development phases. Not because scarcity is a marketing strategy, but because the grounds, the experience, and the community all depend on it. When everyone knows everyone, the culture takes care of itself.

Lowland Sporting Club

A private sporting club rooted in the traditions of the Lowcountry, designed for those who value craftsmanship, community, and the enduring pleasure of days spent outdoors.
